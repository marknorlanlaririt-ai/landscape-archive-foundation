## 2. LinkedIn — long version (founder voice)

> **An open standard for Australian landscape architecture.**
>
> Our profession runs on data — species, soils, microclimates, carbon, cultural context — but it lives in incompatible files, spreadsheets, and proprietary tools. Collaboration between practices, universities, and councils is harder than it should be.
>
> So we're releasing something deliberately *not* owned by any single company: the **Australian Landscape Architecture Federation** open metadata schema.
>
> It's a shared digital language. Any tool — Revit, GIS, CAD, or a spreadsheet — can read and write it. It's free under open licences. And it's designed to be **governed by the community**: a council of practices, universities, councils, and First Nations advisors, with cultural metadata protected by a protocol-authority sign-off.
>
> What's live today:
> • The v1 schema (project, plants, site context, sustainability, Connection to Country)
> • A browser-based validator
> • Worked examples and a Revit crosswalk
> • A draft charter — and a deliberate choice to start lean, with no new company
>
> The Landscape Archive is a founding *implementation partner* — we build tools on the standard, but we hold no vote. The standard belongs to the profession.
>
> If you run a practice, teach landscape architecture, work in a council, or hold cultural authority over Country — **we want you as a founding member.**
>
> Standard, validator, and how to join → https://schema.landscapefederation.org.au

## 4. One-paragraph press / website blurb

> The Australian Landscape Architecture Federation is an open, community-governed metadata standard for describing landscape projects — Indigenous flora, soils and microclimates, climate-positive design, and Connection to Country — using open web standards (JSON Schema / JSON-LD) that any BIM, CAD, or GIS tool can read. Free under Apache-2.0 and CC BY 4.0, it is designed so practices, universities, councils, and Traditional Owner organisations can collaborate without lock-in, while software vendors implement the standard as neutral partners.

## 6. FAQ deflectors (for comments)

- **"Is this just The Landscape Archive's product?"** No — it's an open standard under open licences. LA is one implementation partner with no vote. Anyone can build on it.
- **"Do I have to pay?"** No. The schema and validator are free.
- **"Does joining affect my practice's independence?"** No. The federation governs only the shared schema. Your staff, clients, fees, designs, and software stay yours.
- **"How is Connection to Country handled?"** With a sensitivity classification and a First Nations protocol-authority sign-off that majority votes cannot override.
